Section 01 · Executive Summary

The shape of the field

What the Beacon corpus actually contains — and what it can resolve.

AI is now the dominant U.S. tech-policy story across every monitored ideology. In the last fourteen days alone, the Beacon news layer logged 3,641 AI- and data-center-related headlines. Across the longer corpus — Twitter back to 2013, podcasts since 2024, news since July 2025 — the platform has surfaced over 61,000 AI-tagged articles, 2,600 podcast chunks, and 2,348 tweets across six ideological buckets. The question is not whether Beacon can map the field. It is which voices get the highest leverage from each bucket — and where we are still flying blind.

Volume tilts right; reach tilts further right

MAGA accounts have logged the most AI tweets (292), with Dissident Right close behind (241). But measured by impressions, those two buckets — driven by Alex Jones, Andrew Tate, and Cernovich — produce roughly five-to-ten times the reach of left-coded counterparts.

Strong · n=2,348 tweets

Podcast layer is Beacon's deepest signal

All-In and Bannon's War Room together account for 482 AI-tagged chunks — nearly a fifth of the entire podcast layer. Cross-ideology spread is good (Democracy Now! and Tucker Carlson both present) but academic and AI-safety podcasts are absent.

Strong · 15+ shows indexed

Elected officials are the structural blind spot

Congress is where AI policy gets written, but the Beacon Twitter corpus carries only sparse coverage of senators and representatives — Cruz (15 tweets), Cornyn (18), Foushee (14). Schumer, Hawley, Lieu, Warner, Klobuchar are effectively invisible. Elected officials are a hard data gap.

Verified · gap memo

Left intellectual depth is thin

The Far Left bucket captures outlets (Jacobin, Intercept, DropSite) but not the thinkers who define progressive AI critique — Whittaker, Gebru, Khan, Marx, Bender, Doctorow are not monitored. The KB has zero dedicated AI-policy think tanks (Brookings, CSIS, AEI, Heritage AI). Web investigation will need to backfill.

Section 04 · Ideology × AI Stance

The orthogonal map

Stance on AI doesn't track partisan lines. Plotting ideology against AI position reveals four populated quadrants — and the most influential single account anchors the “right accelerationist” corner.

Motion Chart 07 · Quadrant Scatter
X axis: political ideology, left ↔ right. Y axis: AI stance, skeptical ↔ accelerationist. Dot size = reach.

The accelerationist axis is bipartisan

Reid Hoffman, Mark Cuban, and Noah Smith on the center-left share an upper plane with David Sacks, Jason Calacanis, and Elon Musk on the center-right. The All-In Podcast is functionally the median voice of this axis. Pivot/Galloway sits one notch lower — accelerationist with reservations.

Strong

The skeptic quadrant pairs Jacobin with Steve Bannon

The lower-left and lower-right corners hold ideologically opposite voices that nevertheless converge on AI critique: corporate concentration, labor displacement, surveillance overreach, and China hawkishness. Same content, different rhetorical packaging.

Strong
Section 05 · Extremes Converge

Where the curve closes

Adapting the Horseshoe Convergence framework — the same lens we built for antisemitism research — to AI policy critique. Far Left and Dissident Right anchors arrive at six shared talking points by very different routes.

Motion Chart 13 · Horseshoe Convergence Gauge
The arc fills from both ends inward; six convergence themes settle into the bottom of the curve.

This is testable, not rhetorical

Each of the six convergence themes maps to a Beacon query the platform can run today: paired-quote retrieval across Jacobin and Bannon's War Room, lexical overlap scoring, citation co-incidence on regulatory bills. The horseshoe is a map, not a metaphor — it tells us where to point the system.

Method

Center-bucket voices do not converge

Mainstream Dem and Mainstream GOP voices populate the upper arc, far from each other and far from the convergence floor. The horseshoe geometry reflects observed lexical distance — the ends are closer to each other on AI than either is to the political middle.

Strong

Section 08 · Key Findings

What this brief actually establishes

Six findings with evidence tier, organized for quick handoff to the research planner.

1 · Beacon is mappable across all six buckets

Even in the thinnest bucket (Mainstream GOP, 139 tweets · 23 accounts) the platform has enough sample to surface representative voices and recurring frames. The six-bucket framework is operational.

Strong · n=2,348

2 · Right-coded reach exceeds left-coded reach by ~6×

Ranking accounts by total impressions, MAGA + Dissident Right combined account for roughly 80% of total reach in the dataset, despite being only 47% of the accounts and 23% of the tweets.

Strong

3 · The accelerationist coalition is bipartisan-ish

The high-reach pro-AI cluster pulls Reid Hoffman, Mark Cuban, Noah Smith, David Sacks, Jason Calacanis, and Elon Musk into the same upper-quadrant region — separated horizontally but materially aligned. Standing pattern in the corpus, not a one-week artifact.

Strong

4 · Far Left and Dissident Right share six AI critiques

Big-tech monopoly power, labor displacement, surveillance overreach, China hawkishness, capital concentration, and cultural homogenization all surface in both ends of the spectrum. Lexical proximity is testable directly with paired-quote retrieval.

Mod · pending verification

5 · Elected officials are a structural blind spot

The Twitter corpus carries fragments of Cruz, Cornyn, and Foushee; nothing from Schumer, Hawley, Lieu, Klobuchar, Warner, Gallagher, Moolenaar, or other AI-relevant policymakers. Web Investigator has been allocated specifically for backfill.

Verified · gap memo

6 · Left intellectual depth requires web backfill

Whittaker, Gebru, Khan, Marx, Bender, and Doctorow are all unmonitored. The Far Left tag captures outlets, not thinkers. No Brookings, CSIS, AEI, or Heritage AI-policy material indexed. Cannot be fixed at platform level — has to come through web research.

Section 09 · Methodology & Gaps

How to read this

Evidence tiers, scope constraints, and the six known limitations to flag with any consumer of the brief.

TierSource classConfidence basis
STRONGBeacon podcast + Twitter corporan > 100 in both layers, multiple independent sources, time-series visible across > 6 months
MODSingle-layer Beacon claimsn > 50 in one layer; corroborated by news search but not yet cross-verified by claim verifier
VERIFIEDCounted facts (account counts, chunk counts)Direct queries against Beacon DB at brief assembly time
GAPKnown absence in monitored corpusAsserted only as a gap, not as a substantive claim

Limitations

  1. News DB starts July 2025. Pre-DeepSeek (Jan 2025) news coverage is not available in the news layer — Twitter and podcast layers extend back further.
  2. Elon Musk is untagged. 34 tweets and 147M views sit outside the six-bucket taxonomy. The dashboard renders him grey; bucket assignment is the highest-priority tagging fix.
  3. Elected officials thin. Schumer, Hawley, Lieu, Warner, Klobuchar, Gallagher, Moolenaar effectively absent from the Twitter corpus. Backfill via Web Investigator only.
  4. No dedicated AI-policy think tanks. Brookings Tech, CSIS, AEI, Heritage, FAS material is not in the KB. Web research only.
  5. Far Left = outlets, not thinkers. Jacobin, Intercept, DropSite are present; Whittaker, Gebru, Khan, Marx, Bender, Doctorow are not. The bucket needs intellectual-figure expansion.
  6. No AI-safety podcasts. 80,000 Hours, Dwarkesh, AI X-risk material not in the podcast index. Likely affects how stark the AI-skeptic axis appears.
