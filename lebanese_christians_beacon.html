A multi-source intelligence layer over a standard Claude report — what an LLM-only summary missed, and what only Beacon can verify with timestamped quotes, engagement data, and cross-platform corroboration.

Claude produced a plausible-looking biographical brief on nine Lebanese Christian figures. Beacon produces a verified one. Every claim in this report links to a real source in the Beacon database. Sources are tagged for ideology and credibility. Where data is absent, it is marked — not papered over.

03Position SpectrumThree tiers

Three tiers of Lebanese Christian pro-Israel positioning

Across the cohort, positions cluster into three discrete tiers. Tier 1 voices openly call for alliance, normalization, or Israeli sovereignty. Tier 2 voices support direct negotiation and Hezbollah disarmament without endorsing Israeli military operations. Tier 3 voices remain pastoral — calling for peace through scriptural and prayerful framing without direct Israel positioning.

09Tier ArchetypesFive role types

Five archetypes inside the three tiers

Within the position tiers, voices play different functional roles in the broader narrative ecosystem. Some are diaspora amplifiers; some are institutional pastoralists; some are direct-access lobbyists with no public reach but real power. Mapping role types is more useful than counting positions.

10Anti-PatternsWhat virality looks like

The high-engagement edge of the spectrum

Engagement data tells you which messages are landing — not which positions are mainstream. The top-performing posts in this dataset come from the most maximalist voices. That asymmetry, between political modesty and viral reach, is itself a finding.
