01 · The Spectrum

Six bands. Four hundred fifty-seven voices.

The American political conversation, as it shows up on X, is not a continuum — it is a set of distinct camps with their own institutional trust, media diets, and rhetoric. We sort our monitored accounts into six bands, from Far Left to Dissident Right.

05 · Reach Asymmetry

Posting volume is not reach.

A handful of mega-accounts dominate. MAGA and Dissident Right combined produce 9.9 billion 90-day views — more than the four other bands combined. Traditional Democrats post the least but punch above their volume on raw views, driven by accounts like Obama, Clinton, and Pelosi.

06 · The Horseshoe

A line, bent at the ends.

Read as a straight line, the spectrum implies the wings are as far apart as possible. Read as a horseshoe — the more honest shape — the wings curve toward each other, sharing rhetorical moves: anti-establishment populism, anti-mainstream-media epistemics, conspiratorial framings of foreign policy, and contempt for the political center.

What the wings share.

The 19-dimension scorecard makes the convergence concrete: deep institutional distrust, full conspiracism, rejection of consensus expertise, and a populism that frames opponents as illegitimate.

07 · Method

How we built it.

The scorecard is a research artifact. The data layer is live Beacon — every account, every post, every view count is queried against the production database at render time.

Source

Beacon AI

Beacon is the in-house narrative-intelligence platform built by VFT Institute. It ingests X posts, podcast transcripts, and news articles for monitored entities, with structured tagging across category, role, tier, network, and political-alignment dimensions.

Tags

Political-Alignment Taxonomy

Six tags on the political-alignment dimension — Far Left, Progressive Left, Traditional Democrat, Traditional Republican, MAGA, Dissident Right — applied by analysts after multi-source review of stated positions, retweet patterns, and venue history.

Window

Rolling 90 days

Engagement metrics (posts, views, likes, retweets) cover the trailing 90 days from the report build date. Follower counts are snapshot at last sync. Weekly volume in §04 covers the 12 complete weeks ending Apr 26, 2026.

Limitations

  1. Tag assignment is a research judgment, not a self-identified label. Bands are best understood as ideological centers of gravity, not categorical fits — figures like JD Vance or RFK Jr. could plausibly sit in adjacent bands.
  2. The 19-dimension scorecard describes positions as articulated by representative voices in each band; not every individual account holds every position attributed to its band.
  3. Follower counts are snapshot values from the last platform sync and may lag intra-week. View counts are X-reported and have known platform-side gaps.
  4. The 457-account roster reflects what Beacon currently monitors — it is not a population census of American political X. The set is weighted toward voices that drive narrative across our partner verticals.
